Wildlife doesn't respect borders.
Neither does this map.

SpeciesMap is an open platform that brings together sighting records, GPS tracking data, and conservation assessments from the world's leading scientific databases — in one place, with one interface.

We don't collect data. We connect it. Every sighting you see is sourced from GBIF, Movebank, eBird, or the IUCN Red List — then normalised, mapped, and made navigable for anyone from a curious school student to a field researcher.

This is a passion project, built in the open. The platform has the potential to scale significantly with proper infrastructure investment — covering all wildlife classes including the 2 billion+ bird observations in GBIF — but for now we focus on doing one thing exceptionally well.

How it works

01 — Collect
Data from the source

We pull sighting records directly from GBIF, Movebank, eBird, and the IUCN Red List. Updated regularly with automated sync jobs.

02 — Compute
Biodiversity intelligence

Raw sightings are processed into H3 hex grids, scored for biodiversity, and analysed for temporal trends — turning data into insight.

03 — Visualise
Maps that tell stories

Interactive biodiversity maps, animated migration routes, species range overlays, and conservation status — built for exploration at every level.

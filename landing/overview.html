The opportunity

Environmental data already exists. The missing layer is product quality.

Billions of wildlife observations, conservation records, and tracking datasets are already available. Most remain difficult to navigate, visually weak, and inaccessible outside research workflows. SpeciesMap turns that raw complexity into a product people can actually explore.

What exists now

Past prototype. Entering scale.

  • FastAPI backend serving species, sightings, routes, and biodiversity endpoints
  • Vue-based map application with biodiversity and sightings modes
  • PostGIS-backed geospatial data model
  • ETL pipeline normalizing biodiversity records into one product system
Why now

Data availability is rising faster than interface quality.

  • Open biodiversity data continues to expand globally
  • Climate and ecosystem visibility are becoming more urgent
  • Map-based interfaces can now present this data meaningfully
  • A real working foundation already exists and can be accelerated
Infrastructure reality

The current server proves the thesis. It does not represent the ceiling.

Today the platform runs on a small VPS with 4 vCPU and 4 GB RAM. That is enough for development, selective live usage, and validating the architecture. It is not enough for high-throughput geospatial workloads, richer continent rollouts, or the kind of responsive public product this can become.
